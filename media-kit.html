EXHIBIT I

ABOUT THE SHOW

The People vs. Lee Harvey Oswald is a 12-episode mock trial podcast set in 1965 — a world where Oswald survived Jack Ruby’s bullet and finally faces trial for the assassination of President John F. Kennedy.

All testimony and evidence is drawn from the official record: the 26 volumes of the Warren Commission, contemporary witness statements, and forensic findings available in 1965. No conspiracy theories. No modern speculation. Just the facts as the jury would have heard them.

The podcast invites listeners to sit on the jury and decide the verdict — the trial history denied us finally begins.

EXHIBIT III

EPISODE FORMAT

Each episode follows the structure of a real criminal trial, from jury selection through closing arguments. The “Rule of 1965” governs all proceedings: only evidence and testimony available in 1965 is admissible.

  • 01 Opening Statements — The Prosecution’s “Unsurmountable Mountain”
  • 02 The Crime Scene — Dealey Plaza reconstructed through witness testimony
  • 03 The Rifle — Forensic and ballistic evidence examined
  • 04–08 Witness testimony, cross-examination, and forensic analysis
  • 09–10 The Defense presents their case
  • 11 Closing arguments from both sides
  • 12 Jury deliberation & the verdict — YOU decide
